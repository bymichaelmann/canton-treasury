Smart Contracts

4 Daml templates, purpose-built

TreasuryBill

CIP-056 UTXO-based token. Supports Transfer, Split, Merge — mirroring real T-Bill custody with maturity dates and CUSIP identifiers.

TransferRestriction

Enforces rules on every transfer: min accreditation (Accredited Investor / QIB), max holding, jurisdiction blacklist. In-contract, not in middleware.

InvestorRegistry

On-chain KYC/AML lifecycle. Compliance officer registers investors, sets accreditation, updates KYC status: Pending → Approved / Rejected / Expired.

PrivateDataRoom

Confidential document sharing. Only the issuer and explicitly authorized investors see the document. Canton privacy — if you're not named, the contract doesn't appear on your ledger.

Why this fits perfectly

RWA Tokenization

T-Bills are the most straightforward RWA — $27T outstanding. Our UTXO model maps directly to how custodians hold Treasuries.

Privacy for Institutions

If a pension fund buys $50M in T-bills, they don't want that public. Canton's model means only relevant parties see the transaction.

Compliance Automation

TransferRestriction checks accreditation, KYC, jurisdiction, and holding limits on every single transfer at the contract level.

Real Deals Need Data Rooms

Our PrivateDataRoom solves a real institutional need — you can't do a Reg D offering without sharing docs only with qualified investors.

Demo Flow

End-to-end workflow

  • 1. Register investor — Compliance officer adds investor to registry with accreditation level
  • 2. Approve KYC — Officer approves the investor's KYC status
  • 3. Mint T-Bill — Issuer creates a CIP-056 TreasuryBill token for a specific party
  • 4. Transfer with compliance — Transfer automatically checked against TransferRestriction rules
  • 5. Split / Merge — Split a holding into two UTXOs, merge back — mirrors real custody
  • 6. Private Data Room — Share deal docs only with qualified investors they authorize
